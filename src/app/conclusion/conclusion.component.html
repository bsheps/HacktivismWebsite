Based on the points raise, we believe Hacktivism provides significant value to society.

If we look through the lense of a philosopher like Immanual Kant, I think he would also agree that hacktivism has a place in society. He would likely condone the actions of the few bad apples operating under hacktivist group names. We think he would also argue that hacktivism is not only good overall, but is a moral obligation and duty of those who are technologically inclined. Furthermore, if we did decide to dismiss hacktivism as pure evil and banish it from society, who would fill the societal void currently held by hacktivists?

Without hacktivists who would hold the powerful accountable?

Who would expose unethical behavior of corporations and governments?

Who would give suppressed people a voice?

We are unable to find a better solution to these questions, and while there have been some questionable hacktivist movements in the past we can't let a few bad apples tarnish the lot.
